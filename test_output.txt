## 4. Cultural Compass: Decorum and The Soul of Miyako

The cultural experience of Kyoto is defined by patience and respect for history (*Miyako*).

### Cultural Wisdom: Quiet Observation and Mono no Aware

The essence of Kyoto’s autumn is **Mono no Aware** (the poignant beauty of transient things). The intense red of the maple leaf is beautiful precisely because it is fleeting.

*   **Your Role:** You are a participant in a meditative tradition, not just a tourist. Approach the gardens and temple viewing areas with quiet reverence. **Do not use loud voices.**
*   **Photography:** Be quick and considerate. Do not block main pathways or doorways for extended periods. Step aside immediately after capturing your image.

### Essential Decorum

1.  **Temple Entrance:** Always give a slight bow when passing through a *Torii* gate (shrine) or a *Sanmon* gate (temple).
2.  **Purification:** If using a *Temizuya* (water font), purify your left hand, then right hand, then mouth (spitting the rinse water outside the basin), and finally the ladle handle, ensuring the spout never touches your mouth.
3.  **Shoes and Hats:** **MANDATORY:** Remove shoes, hats, and sunglasses before stepping onto the wooden verandas (*engawa*) or entering the main halls of any temple. Place shoes neatly in the provided areas.
